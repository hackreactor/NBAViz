Q: Where do the rankings come from?

A: The rankings are calculated by normalizing every available NBA stat, then allowing users to assign a weight to that stat.

Q: What is normalizing?

A: Our Normalizing process:
1. Calculate the per minute rate of a stat like, say, steals.
2. Look at the whole league, find the best rate of steals/minute, the worst, and the range.
3. Assign everyone a number from -1 to +1 , 1 being best in league, -1 being worst, 0 being average.

Q: So where do the player scores come from?

A: After we get the normalized number, we add back in the minutes played and multiply by the user-selected (or preset) weights in each statistic, then do a little more math to keep the numbers fairly stable. An average player will score zero no matter how many minutes they play. A good player will get a higher score for playing more minutes, and a bad one will go negative.

Q: What about estimated win percentage?

A: The system returns a number that estimates what percent above or below average the team is. We've found that in most cases, 1% of skill difference amounts to about 3% of win percentage, so we multiply by 3! If the system notices an outlier, it falls back to using pure skill differences.

Q: How is accuracy determined?

A: We use linear regression to compare actual team W/L to Baller W/L.
